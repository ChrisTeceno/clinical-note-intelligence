**CHIEF COMPLAINT:**
Left lower extremity swelling and pain

**HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:**
Patient presented to the hospital with acute onset left lower extremity edema and pain. Imaging studies revealed acute embolism and thrombosis of the left iliac vein with associated vein compression. Given the acute nature of the thrombosis and the patient's clinical deterioration, the decision was made to pursue emergent endovascular intervention. Unfortunately, during the initial catheter-based procedure, an accidental puncture and laceration of a circulatory system organ occurred, which prompted immediate corrective intervention. The complication was managed acutely, and the patient's condition was felt to be directly related to the operative intervention itself rather than any misadventure. The patient remained hemodynamically stable throughout the hospital course despite this intraoperative complication.

**PAST MEDICAL HISTORY:**
Hypothyroidism, depression, dysuria

**HOSPITAL COURSE:**
The patient underwent several percutaneous endovascular procedures to address the left iliac venous thrombosis and associated compression. Initial therapy included dilation of the left common iliac vein with placement of an intraluminal device via percutaneous approach. Thrombolytic therapy was introduced via peripheral vein access, and extirpation of thrombus was performed from both the left femoral vein and left common iliac vein (procedure repeated to ensure complete clearance). An intraluminal device was placed into the inferior vena cava percutaneously for additional thrombotic protection. Finally, the left external iliac vein was dilated with placement of another intraluminal device.

Anticoagulation was initiated with intravenous heparin sodium and alteplase infusions, with doses titrated based on coagulation studies. The patient also received enoxaparin sodium subcutaneously and was transitioned to warfarin with apixaban for long-term anticoagulation coverage. Pain control was achieved with oxycodone immediate release, hydromorphone, and fentanyl infusions, with acetaminophen used as adjunctive therapy. Clonazepam was administered for anxiety management. The patient received levothyroxine sodium for thyroid replacement at 75 mcg daily. Gastrointestinal protection was maintained with ranitidine, and bowel function was supported with polyethylene glycol and docusate sodium. Electrolyte repletion included potassium chloride (both oral and intravenous) and magnesium sulfate. Phenazopyridine was given for dysuria symptoms. Duloxetine was continued at 120 mg daily for depression management. Appropriate fluid resuscitation was provided with normal saline, lactated Ringer's solution, and dextrose-containing intravenous fluids.

Vital signs remained stable throughout the admission, with appropriate pain control achieved. The patient tolerated oral intake by discharge and demonstrated good functional recovery.
