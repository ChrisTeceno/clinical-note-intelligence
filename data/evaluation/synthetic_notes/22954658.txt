**HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:**

**LABORATORY & IMAGING:**
Troponin I peaked at 3.1 ng/mL on day 2. Electrolytes stable throughout admission. Magnesium 1.8 mEq/L. EKG showed persistent ST depressions. Coronary angiography with fluoroscopic imaging of multiple coronary arteries revealed a critical stenosis in the left anterior descending artery with significant thrombus burden. CT angiography confirmed Type B dissection of the abdominal aorta extending from the left subclavian artery; vascular surgery consultation recommended conservative management with close hemodynamic monitoring given the acute MI.

**HOSPITAL COURSE:**
The patient underwent emergent coronary intervention with extirpation of thrombotic material from the left anterior descending artery via percutaneous approach. The procedure was successful with restoration of TIMI 3 flow. Given the dual pathology of acute NSTEMI and aortic dissection, the management strategy emphasized optimization of blood pressure control and heart rate reduction to minimize aortic stress while ensuring adequate coronary perfusion. The patient received IV nitroglycerin infusion, IV hydralazine for blood pressure management, IV magnesium sulfate for cardioprotection, and IV heparin for anticoagulation. Metoprolol tartrate was initiated for beta-blockade. The patient tolerated the procedures well without complications.

Regarding the aortic dissection, vascular surgery determined that conservative medical management was appropriate given the Type B classification and hemodynamic stability. The patient remained stable throughout the hospital stay with no evidence of dissection progression or end-organ ischemia.

As part of preventive care during this admission, the patient received the quadrivalent influenza vaccine.
