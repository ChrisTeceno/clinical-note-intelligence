**REASON FOR ADMISSION:**

**HOSPITAL COURSE:**

This patient with a significant past medical history including essential hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and prior malignant neoplasm of the breast was admitted for elective treatment of a cerebral aneurysm. During the index admission, the pt underwent percutaneous restriction of the left internal carotid artery and intracranial artery using intraluminal devices. 

Unfortunately, the procedures were complicated by several adverse events. The patient developed a postprocedural hemorrhage of a circulatory system organ following the endovascular intervention, with an associated accidental puncture and laceration of a circulatory structure that occurred during the procedure. These complications led to development of hemothorax requiring close monitoring and management. The hemorrhage resulted in acute posthemorrhagic anemia necessitating transfusion support.

Additionally, the pt developed significant cerebral edema in the immediate postoperative period, which was managed with dexamethasone therapy and careful fluid management. The patient also experienced postprocedural hypotension requiring vasopressor and antihypertensive agent titration. Notably, the pt developed postprocedural heart failure following the surgical intervention, compounded by preexisting acute diastolic congestive heart failure, necessitating diuretic use and careful hemodynamic monitoring throughout the stay.

Speech evaluation revealed aphasia related to the cerebrovascular event and swelling. The patient also complained of chest pain during hospitalization, which was appropriately evaluated. Electrolyte disturbances, particularly hypokalemia and hypomagnesemia, were identified and corrected with supplemental repletion. The pt received appropriate seizure prophylaxis with levetiracetam given the risk profile.

Vital signs at discharge were stable with BP ranging 120-140 mmHg systolic, HR 60-75 bpm regular, and O2 saturation on room air at 96%.
