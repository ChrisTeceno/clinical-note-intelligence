**HOSPITAL COURSE:**  
The patient was admitted and started on anticoagulation therapy with heparin sodium IV and enoxaparin sodium subcutaneously. Initial management included pain control with a combination of hydromorphone and oxycodone as needed. The clinical team proceeded with percutaneous endovascular intervention on hospital day 2.

Under fluoroscopic guidance, multiple procedures were performed to address the thrombotic occlusion. Thrombolytic therapy was administered via peripheral vein access with alteplase. Extirpation of thrombus was performed from the left femoral vein, left common iliac vein (performed twice given the burden of clot), and surrounding vessels. Dilation of the left common iliac vein and left external iliac vein was accomplished using intraluminal devices via percutaneous approach to restore adequate flow. An intraluminal device was also inserted into the inferior vena cava for additional protection against recurrent thromboembolic events.

During the procedure, the patient sustained an accidental puncture and laceration of a circulatory system structure, which was recognized and managed appropriately at the time. This complication occurred as a consequence of the surgical procedures being performed and was addressed with conservative management without additional operative intervention required.

Pain was managed with IV hydromorphone initially, then transitioned to oral oxycodone. Anticoagulation was continued with IV heparin and transitioned to warfarin for long-term management. The patient also received apixaban as part of the anticoagulation regimen. Bowel function was supported with polyethylene glycol and docusate sodium.
